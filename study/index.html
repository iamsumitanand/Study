Source Library

Every chapter on this site is derived from these eight authoritative sources. Nothing is synthesised from memory — each factual claim traces back to one of these texts.

A · Part 1 — Basic ML
CS229 Lecture Notes
Andrew Ng & Tengyu Ma, Stanford (2022). The gold standard for ML fundamentals — mathematically rigorous but readable. Covers all of Part 1 from scratch.
cs229.stanford.edu · Free PDF
B · Part 1 — Basic ML
Introduction to Statistical Learning (ISLR)
James, Witten, Hastie & Tibshirani. Best for intuition and visual understanding of ML algorithms. Pairs with CS229 — A for derivations, B for interpretation.
statlearning.com · Free PDF
C · Parts 2–4 — Neural Nets → GenAI
Deep Learning Book
Goodfellow, Bengio & Courville (MIT Press, 2016). The definitive textbook on neural networks and deep learning. Primary source for optimization, backprop, CNNs, RNNs, GANs, VAEs.
deeplearningbook.org · Free online
D · Parts 2–3 — Neural Nets → Deep Learning
Dive into Deep Learning (d2l.ai)
Zhang et al. Code, math, and intuition together — every concept has a runnable implementation. Best for MLPs through transformers. Actively maintained.
d2l.ai · Free online
E · Parts 2–3
CS231n Notes
Stanford (Fei-Fei Li, Karpathy). Purpose-built for CNNs — also the best treatment of backprop, activation functions, and weight initialisation on the web.
cs231n.github.io · Free
F · Parts 4–5 — GenAI → Modern
Lilian Weng's Blog
OpenAI researcher. The single best blog for GANs, VAEs, diffusion models, RLHF, LLM agents, and RAG. Dense and precise — every post is a mini textbook.
lilianweng.github.io · Free
G · Part 3–4 — Transformers → LLMs
The Illustrated Transformer
Jay Alammar. Visual, precise explanations of transformers, attention, BERT, and GPT. Referenced wherever Part 3–4 covers sequence models.
jalammar.github.io · Free
H · Part 5 — Modern GenAI
Anthropic & OpenAI Official Documentation
Primary sources for MCP, prompt engineering, agentic AI, and tool use. No textbook exists for these topics yet — official docs are the authoritative reference. Both are cited side-by-side where they differ.
docs.anthropic.com · Free platform.openai.com/docs · Free
